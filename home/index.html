HOME & HELP PAGE

About this website (for teachers). This website is part of the ISP (Inquiry-based Science Project) Tutor, for middle school students. The content —consistent with NGSS— supports students' background research for common science fair project research questions (e.g., the research question shown above). Students can first view a lesson on selecting a good research question by clicking here. Afterward, they can select their research question by clicking here. (Click on the blank page to start.)

Then students can follow the steps below to find information about their research question.

Important Note: You can also use this website as a resource to find various middle school level science units. You can do this by typing search terms into the search box above. Or you can click here to see a list of all units in this website.

You can also type "glossary" in the search bar to get a list of basic definitions of science concepts.


STEP 1: HELP IN PLANNING YOUR RESEARCH 

For important tips on doing background research, click on the Plan Your Research video button below (on the left).

If you chose the crystal growth experiment, you can click on the Animation button (above right) to watch (or re-watch) an animation for one trial of your experiment to get more ideas about what information to look up.

Tip: Make sure to write down the information you want to look up to help you stay on track.




STEP 2: ONCE YOU HAVE A PLAN FOR WHAT INFORMATION TO LOOK UP...

You can find science units relevant to your research question by clicking on a blue button above for the science area of your research question (e.g., "Forces & Motion"). You'll see a list of units relevant to the topic you chose.

Or, you can type what you would like to find out about in the search bar above (as shown below). Click on the little magnifying glass and you'll see a list of units that include those key words (you may see some Ads in the list of results: just ignore those). Just click on the link of a unit you'd like to look at.




When you've gone to a unit, you may need to click on the orange arrows at the bottom of the page to find the info you're looking for (since many units have more than one page).

For more general tips on how to use this website to find information, click on the button below.

You can always come back to this page by clicking on the "Home & Help" button at the top of the page you're on
(the Help and four Areas of Science buttons will be on every page).

If you still need help and don't know what to do, ask your teacher (or another adult) to help you.
